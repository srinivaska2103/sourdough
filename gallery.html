Chronological Guide

The Sourdough Journey

Track the transformation from wild yeast feeding cycles to the final aerated artisan crumb reveal.

01

Feeding the Starter

Activating the wild yeast colony with equal parts stone-milled organic flour and filtered room temperature water.

02

Mixing the Dough

Combining the active leaven with flour and water for autolayse, before incorporating mineral sea salt blocks.

03

Stretch & Fold

Intermittent physical glass-like fold layers applied over 2 hours to build gluten structure without overworking.

04

Bulk Fermentation

An extended, undisturbed ambient rest block allowing carbon dioxide gas pockets to aerate the interior matrix.

05

Shaping the Loaf

Creating structural surface tension by rolling the dough into uniform round boules or oblong batards interior matrix.

06

Scoring

Slicing the exterior skin with an ultra-thin razor blade lame to guide the expansion direction inside the heat deck.

07

Baking

Cooking inside a sealed, heavy steam-trapping Dutch oven at high temperatures to maximize oven spring.

08

Final Crumb Reveal

Slicing the cooled loaf open to examine the open-cell structure, glossy walls, and optimal cell distribution.
